Understanding planetary interiors through magnetic and gravitational signals.

I study the hidden interiors of planets by decoding their magnetic and gravitational signals. By combining spacecraft observations with theory and numerical modeling, my work reveals how fluid motion inside planets generates magnetic fields, constrains the depth of atmospheric winds, and provides new insight into planetary structure, evolution, and formation.

Research Areas

From Jupiter and Saturn to Earth and Mercury, these projects use magnetic and gravitational measurements to turn remote observations into insight about planetary interiors.

  • Magnetic Fields of Giant Planets Spacecraft magnetometry and dynamo models reveal how deep electrical structure organizes the magnetic fields of Jupiter and Saturn.
  • Deep Wind Sounding Gravity harmonics constrain how far zonal winds extend below the visible atmosphere.
  • Geomagnetic Polar Minima Core-flow dynamics test how polar magnetic structure and minima emerge inside Earth.
  • Jet-Field Coupling Deep jets shear conducting interiors and leave magnetic signatures that can be observed remotely.
  • Asymmetric Magnetic Field Dynamo-based models explain why Mercury's field is stronger in the north than in the south.

About Hao Cao

Assistant Professor, Department of Earth, Planetary, and Space Sciences, University of California, Los Angeles.

Dr. Hao Cao is an Associate Professor of Space Science at UCLA and PI of the UCLA Space Magnetometer Lab. His research focuses on the space environment and interiors of planetary bodies, including Earth. He has contributed to several flagship NASA and European Space Agency (ESA) missions, serving as the Deputy-PI of the MAG instrument on NASA’s TRACERS mission, Co-Investigator on the ESA JUICE mission to the icy moons of Jupiter, and a member of the NASA Juno mission. He also leads a NASA DALI project developing a next-generation magnetometer for long-term lunar surface operations. His honors include the Sloan Research Fellowship and the NASA Group Achievement Award. "
